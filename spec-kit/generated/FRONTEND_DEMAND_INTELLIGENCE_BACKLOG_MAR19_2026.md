# Demand Intelligence Backlog - Search, Popularity, and Active Skills

**Date**: March 19, 2026

## Purpose
Turn Kelmah into a platform that continuously listens to what users search for, save, hire, and complete so the most searched, used, and active skills stay visible.

## Priority Demand Plan
1. Surface popular search terms on the home page, search page, and empty states.
2. Rank suggestions by live demand instead of only static taxonomy order.
3. Show active job counts and recent hiring velocity on category cards.
4. Add weekly trending badges for skills that are climbing fast.
5. Add monthly demand badges for skills that are consistently hired.
6. Surface popular locations and demand by region for Ghana-first discovery.
7. Blend saved searches, click-through, and saved jobs into recommendation ranking.
8. Add worker-side demand analytics so workers can see where their skills are hottest.
9. Add hirer-side scarcity indicators so low-supply/high-demand jobs stand out.
10. Add an admin view for stale, duplicated, or suspicious demand signals.

## Existing Surfaces To Unify
- Search suggestions and popular terms.
- Popular locations and nearby discovery.
- Smart job recommendations.
- Worker dashboard recommendation blocks.
- Skills assessment analytics and improvement areas.
- Review and rating trends.
- Earnings and performance analytics.

## Demand Gaps To Close
- No single shared shape for trending skills, active jobs, and popular searches.
- Search and recommendation signals are split across services.
- Popularity is not always visible in cards, filters, or empty states.
- Location demand is not consistently tied to trade demand.
- Worker discovery does not yet clearly separate "popular now" from "always needed".
- The platform does not yet explain why a skill is being recommended.

## Trade Coverage Targets
- Plumbing and emergency plumbing.
- Electrical rewiring and switchboard work.
- Carpentry and cabinet work.
- Masonry, roofing, flooring, and construction repair.
- Welding and metal fabrication.
- HVAC, refrigeration, solar, and energy installation.
- Painting, finishing, and surface repair.
- Maintenance and emergency callouts.
- Automotive, transport support, cleaning, and security where demand is active.

## Product Moves
1. Create one shared demand model for skills, jobs, search terms, and locations.
2. Feed demand data into search suggestions, filters, recommendations, and dashboards.
3. Add time windows such as 7-day, 30-day, and 90-day demand views.
4. Add explanatory text like "popular this week" or "most hired in Accra".
5. Add fallback states that still show useful demand hints when live analytics are unavailable.
6. Make demand signals mobile-first so the hottest jobs are visible without scrolling far.

## Acceptance Notes
- A user should be able to see what is popular now without hunting across several screens.
- A worker should understand which skills are active in the market before applying.
- A hirer should quickly find scarce, high-demand workers and jobs.
- The system should degrade cleanly when analytics endpoints are offline.
